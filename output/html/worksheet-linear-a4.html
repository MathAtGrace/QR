The average rate of change you just found between points \(A\) and \(B\) is the slope of the line connecting the two points.

It means that the function drops about \(-1\) unit for every unit we move to the right, on average. But this is only if the inputs are in the interval between \(x = 2\) and \(x = 7\text{.}\)

Here is how you say that in math:

\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x} \approx -1 \text{ for }2 \leq x \leq 7 \end{equation*}
